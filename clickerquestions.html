Topic 19: Confidence Interval for the Population Mean

Outcome A: Find the confidence interval for the population mean.
T19.A1

A light bulb manufacturer tested 40 randomly sampled light bulbs and recorded the time until burn out. The sample mean is 740 hours and the sample standard deviation is 25 hours. What is the critical value for a 95% confidence interval?

1. 2.030 25%
2. 2.021 25%
3. 1.960 25%
4. 1.684 25%
Outcome D: Verify the conditions for conducting inference for a population mean are met.
T19.D1

According to CreditCard.com, the average American has voer $9,300 in credit card debt by some estimates.

Do you think the sampling distribution of average credit card debt based on a random sample of 10 people is nearly normal? (Hint: Think about the distribution of credit card debt by all Americans.)

Note: About 25% have no credit cards, and an additional 30% or so pay off their balances every month.

Source: Federal Reserve Board survey of consumer finances, 2004.
